About CBSE Curriculum

The Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) is an examination board that conducts examinations at Secondary School, and Senior Secondary School. It aligns with the National Testing Agency that conducts the graduation and post-graduation tests (NTA) (JEE, NEET, CMAT, GPAT, NET). As per the new National Education Policy (NEP 2020).

Schools that offer the CBSE examinations follow the National Curriculum Framework (NCF 2023) prescribed by the National Council for Educational Research and Training (NCERT) to create their indigenous curriculum. Recognised as one of the CBSE syllabus schools in Hyderabad, Sanskriti The School, Dilsukhnagar, seamlessly blends its philosophy with the standards and practices enshrined within the National Education Policy (2020).

Benefits of the CBSE Programme

The CBSE program offers several advantages for our students:

  • Strong Foundation in Core Subjects: The curriculum prepares a base in core subjects like mathematics, science, and languages, equipping students for success in higher education and future careers.
  • Holistic Development: The CBSE program emphasizes well-rounded development by including sports, arts, and a variety of extracurricular activities. This holistic approach ensures that students develop not just their intellectual abilities but also their physical, social, and emotional well-being.
  • National Recognition and Seamless Transitions: The CBSE program is recognized by Indian colleges and universities throughout the country facilitating a smooth transition for students from high school to higher education.
  • Preparation for Competitive Exams: The CBSE curriculum also prepares students for national-level competitive exams, such as JEE (Joint Entrance Examination) and NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test), crucial for pursuing careers in engineering and medicine.
  • Alignment with National Educational Standards: The CBSE curriculum is aligned with the National Curriculum Framework (NCF) and the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020. This ensures that our students receive an education that meets the highest national standards.
