How a Holistic Curriculum Changes the Way Your Child Thinks

admin
6 years ago

Education is not solely about learning concepts of Math or Physics and obtaining high grades. Education means learning to think for yourself, learning not to be scared with uncertainty or difficulty, learning to accept the point of view of others, and gaining core skills and the ability to decide what one wants to do in his life.

From a child’s perspective; academics, extracurricular activities, and home experience are integrated and together play a role in the growth process. However, parents, facilitators, and coaches seem to look at each segment separately. That’s where the fault lies in the system.
The adults in a child’s life need to collaborate in all the spheres of development and follow a clear path of growth. A holistic approach offers an experience filled with accomplished goals. It opens doors to the development of a well-rounded adult.
This blog unfolds specific ways in which holistic curriculum changes the way a child thinks. Have a look at its benefits.

1. Quest for Life Long Learning


Holistic education makes learning enjoyable and meaningful. The curriculum fuels the child’s quest for lifelong learning and ensures the child seeks to discover beyond the four walls of the classroom.

2. Spurs Creativity and Imagination


Holistic curriculum propagates play-based learning, wherein a child’s creativity and imagination find space and utility in traditional subjects. It creates an environment for children to explore and experiment. For instance, a drama or speech when included in a lesson offers experiential learning.
Such connections encourage the child to use their creative skills to solve practical problems in future as well.

3. Develops Intrinsic Motivation


A holistic curriculum helps improve communication and social skills in children, thereby building their self-confidence. Developing one’s own voice is important. The ideology aims at igniting intrinsic motivation that steers curiosity and propels the development of an innovative adult.

4. Promotes Self Reflection


An integrated system of education motivates students to develop insights about themselves, their interests and values, and their future roles in society.
This progressive approach towards learning helps children learn more about who they are, what they believe in, and what are their opinions about political, religious, and philosophical issues. The self- reflection triggered resonates with them for years to come.

5. Teaches Perseverance


In a world of fast food and inactivity, a diverse physical education programme helps embrace a healthy lifestyle.
Students develop self-discipline. They learn valuable lessons in team building and co-operation that come handy in professional domains.

6. Promotes Emotional Health


If boredom, frustration, and despair set in; no education can succeed. Emotional health is crucial for success. Under-confidence, anxiety, depression, and uncertainty cripple an individual’s ability to share their knowledge with others.
Positivity gives way to productivity. Raising resilient and mentally healthy children is the essence of holistic learning. A student must see himself as someone who can offer the world something special; else knowledge acquired will fail to reproduce tangible goals.

7. Responsible Citizenship


Compulsory community service develops a sense of responsible citizenship. Children learn to look beyond themselves and learn ways to contribute to the surroundings in which they live and grow.
A holistic curriculum produces empowered citizens of the world. It cements their connections to the community, natural world and humanitarian values.

Summary

Holistic education endeavours to teach in a more natural and engaging format. It forbids compartmentalisation of school subjects and skills. A comprehensive curriculum empowers students to use their cognitive knowledge as a foothold for emotional and social development.
Sanskriti School aims at creating ‘whole’ individuals who are well balanced in their outlook towards life. The ideology of the school emphasises that there is learning everywhere and in everything we do. The holistic curriculum followed in the school ignites a passionate love for learning and helps find purpose in life through connections with the society and humanitarian ethics.
